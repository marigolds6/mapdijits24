Find Your Location

  1. Center on your location
      Unincorporated or Contract Munis
    1. Select your current Beat Plan
    2. Select your Beat
    3. Map will automatically zoom into your beat

      Other areas
    • Move mouse over the map
    • Zoom in with +
    • Zoom out with -
    • Move around the map with the arrow keys
    • Zoom to a box by holding down shift and touch/click and dragging. You should see a red box that will be your zoom area.
    • You can also zoom in and out using the slider on the left hand side of the screen.

    2. Need to zoom back out to start?
    Click the globe button.
    Want to see aerial photos with the property boundaries and roads?
    1. In the top right corner of the map, click on "Switch Basemap". This will expand the basemap selection window.
    2. Click on "Satellite" to turn on aerial photos (currently from 2010). To switch back to street map only, select "Street".
    3. Click on "Switch Basemap" again to minimize the basemap selection window.

  2. Zoom to edit level to record damage
    • Do this by clicking the Zoom to parcel button.
    • You can zoom in closer and still edit, but if you zoom out farther, editing will be disabled until you zoom back in.

Record Damage

You must be at one of the Edit zoom levels (top 4 notches on the zoom slider).
If you are zoomed out too far, damage recording will be disabled.

To change the record for a property, tap it once.
    As you touch each property, it cycles through three states.
  1. Green - No Damage.
  2. Red - Damage. This includes all levels of damage from shingles missing to house levelled.
  3. Blue - Clear record. ONLY USE THIS TO CORRECT MISTAKES.
Double-clicking/double-tapping will advance the property two steps on the cycle.

Select by line
    You can also change a whole group of properties at once.
  1. Touch "Select by Line" and the button changes to "Drawing..." to show you are in draw mode.
  2. Trace a line over the properties you want to change. Each property touched by the line will cycle up one state.
  3. The line can loop and overlap itself, but will only advance each property once per trace.
      4. Example:
    • Trace a line through two clear properties and two green properties.
    • The two clear properties will change to green and the two green properties to red.
      Example:
    • To mark an entire clear block damaged, choose Select by Line and trace through each property in the block. All the properties will turn green.
    • Choose Select by Line again and trace through all the green properties again. All of those properties will now turn blue.
  4. You can correct mistakes by clicking individual properties until they are at the right state.
Sending reports
    Once you have finished recording damaged and undamaged properties, you still need to send your reports.
  1. Click the "Send Reports" button.
  2. Next to the button you will see a message that says "Sending # Reports.", where # is the number of properties you have changed.
  3. When the changes are sent, that message will change to "Done." Wait until you see this message before recording more damage.

Troubleshooting

Properties are not changing color
  • The map services are down. Ask dispatch to notify Emergency Management or send an email to Brett Lord-Castillo.
  • In an emergency, a police staff message will be sent out when the services are back up.
  • In testing, you will be notified directly.
Arrow keys not working
  • Make sure the mouse is centered over the map and not the top banner or zoom slider.
Send reports fails
  • You should always do your first "Send Reports" after your first entry. If the send fails, wait 30 seconds to and second time (the report service might have been initializing).
  • If the send fails a second time, ask dispatch to notify Emergency Management or send an email to Brett Lord-Castillo.
